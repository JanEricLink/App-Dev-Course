Ruleset

Goal

Every player tries to get 10 VP (Victory Points) by building settlements, roads and cities.

Components

  • Players: 3–4 players (2–6 with expansions)
  • Game pieces: 95 pieces (19 hexes, 6 harbors, 18 settlements, 14 cities, 60 roads, 1 robber)
  • Cards: 25 development cards (14 knight cards, 6 progress cards, 5 victory point cards), 95 resource cards (19 each of brick, lumber, wool, grain, ore)

Setup

Board

The game board is made up of hexagonal tiles that represent different types of terrain (forest, hills, mountains, fields, pasture, and desert). The tiles are arranged randomly to create a unique board for each game.

Players

Each player chooses a color and takes the corresponding pieces. They place two settlements and two roads on the board. The first settlement is placed in player turn order; the second settlement is placed in reverse turn order (snake draft). After placement, players collect resource cards for any terrain hexes adjacent to their second settlement.

Game Rules

The following rules describe the full turn structure and key gameplay systems in Catan (Base Game, 3–4 players):

  1. Start of Turn – Dice Roll
    • The active player rolls two six-sided dice (2D6).
    • Each terrain hex with a matching number produces resources for all players who have a settlement or city adjacent to that hex.
    • Settlements yield 1 resource card; cities yield 2 resource cards.
    • If a 7 is rolled:
      • No resources are produced.
      • Every player holding more than 7 resource cards must discard half (rounded down).
      • The active player moves the Robber to a new hex and steals 1 random resource card from a player with an adjacent settlement or city.
  2. Trading Phase (Optional)
    • The active player may trade resources with other players or with the bank.
    • Domestic Trade: Players negotiate freely; trades must be agreed upon by both sides.
    • Maritime Trade: The player may trade with the bank using:
      • 4:1 ratio — any four identical resources for one of any type.
      • 3:1 ratio — if the player has a generic harbor.
      • 2:1 ratio — if the player has a specialized harbor for that resource.
  3. Building Phase (Optional)
    • Players may spend resource cards to build or upgrade structures according to the following costs:
      • Road — 1 Brick + 1 Lumber
      • Settlement — 1 Brick + 1 Lumber + 1 Grain + 1 Wool
      • City (upgrade) — 3 Ore + 2 Grain
      • Development Card — 1 Ore + 1 Grain + 1 Wool
    • Building restrictions:
      • Roads must connect to your existing network.
      • Settlements must be at least two edges away from any other settlement.
      • Each city replaces one of your settlements (you return the settlement piece to your supply).
  4. Development Cards
    • Purchased during the Building Phase; kept secret until played.
    • Types of Development Cards:
      • Knight: Move the Robber and steal one resource card.
      • Progress Cards:
        • Road Building — place two free roads.
        • Year of Plenty — take any two resource cards from the supply.
        • Monopoly — claim all of one resource type from other players.
      • Victory Point Cards: Worth 1 VP each; revealed only when counting for victory.
  5. Special Achievements
    • Longest Road (2 VP): Awarded to the player with the longest continuous road of at least 5 segments.
    • Largest Army (2 VP): Awarded to the player who has played at least 3 Knight cards.
    • These bonuses can change ownership during the game.
  6. Victory Condition
    • The first player to reach 10 Victory Points (VP) during their turn immediately wins the game.
    • Victory Points are earned through:
      • Settlements — 1 VP each
      • Cities — 2 VP each
      • Longest Road — 2 VP
      • Largest Army — 2 VP
      • Victory Point Development Cards — 1 VP each
